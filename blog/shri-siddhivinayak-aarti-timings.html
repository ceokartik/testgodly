The usual Aarti timings in Shri Siddhivinayak Temple

The following are the daily schedule of Shri Siddhivinayak Aarti timings. Keep in mind that the idol (the correct Sanskrit word for the idol is Vigraha) is like a person we can serve.   • The first Aarti in Siddhivinayak temple starts at 5:30 am to 6:00 am. Devotees take darshan in the early morning during Kakad Aarti. You can look for Siddhivinayak Kakad Aarti lyrics in Hindi.
  • From 6:00 am to 12:15 pm, devotees take darshan of Bappa. One has to line up in an always-moving queue. The devotees believe walking patiently in the queue for long hours is like a test of faith.
  • 12:15 pm is the time for lunch for Bappa called Naivedya, and it lasts till 12:30 pm. The temple doors remain closed while devotees offer the food to the idol of Lord Ganesha.
  • Devotees can take darshan from 12:30 pm to 7:20 pm; then comes the time slot of 7:30 pm to 8:00 pm for evening Aarti.
  • Ganpati Bappa gives the final darshan of the day from 8 pm to 9:50 pm.
  • The day ends with Shej Aarti at 9:50 pm. The temple remains closed after Shejaarti.

Check out Shri Siddhivinayak Aarti Timings on Tuesday

  • On Tuesday, Siddhivinayak temple opens sharply from 3:15 am to 4:45 am. You can also take darshan during this time. Many devotees in Mumbai take padayatra (walking journey) for this special darshan on Tuesday.

Why should you watch Siddhivinayak Live Aarti on Tuesday?

In Hindu culture, specific days in a week belong to some gods. Tuesday is said to be a day when devotees of Lord Ganesha prefer to fast. Like every Hindu God, Lord Ganesha is the Lord of several divine qualities.
In Marathi, one of Siddhivinayak aarti lyrics addresses Lord Ganesha, "Sukh Karta and Dukh Harta," which means giver of happiness and remover of sorrow. He is also said to be the embodiment of auspiciousness. Mangalwar is a Sanskrit translation of Tuesday; the word Mangal means auspicious.
If you try to corroborate the above reasons, you can understand why Lord Ganesha is extra merciful on this particular day. The schedule of Shree Siddhivinayak Aarti on Vinayaka Chaturthi

  • Kakad Aarti takes place from 5:30 am to 6 am. The darshan timing starts from 6 am to 7:30 am. As you can notice, the darshan at this time is shorter than the other days.
  • The time slot of 7:30 am to 1:00 pm is for Abhishek, Naivedya, and Pooja Aarti. No devotees are allowed during this special ceremony to Lord Ganesha.
  • Devotees can take darshan of Bappa from 1:00 pm to 7:20 pm; one can attend evening Aarti at 7:30 pm that goes on till 8:00 pm.
  • Visitors get the final opportunity to take darshan of Ganpati Bappa from 8:00 pm to 9:15 pm.
  • With Shej Aarti at 9:50 pm, the temple remains closed till the following day.

Keep in mind Vinayak Chaturthi lasts for ten days as it is one of the most loved festivals in Mumbai. Millions of devotees visit Siddhivinayak temple during these ten days. The significance of the Shri Siddhivinayak temple

India is the land of spirituality. In every street and corner, you will come across a temple. Many highly enlightened souls have heralded India as the spiritual superpower of the world. No wonder this ancient land of wisdom has enamored several spiritual seekers.

One of such holy places in the city that never sleeps is the Shri Siddhivinayak temple Mumbai. Millions of devotees and visitors go to this temple yearly. The government documents state the contractor Laxman Vithu Patil built this temple on November 19, 1801. Since then, Siddhivinayak mandir has become one of the most popular holy sites in Mumbai, visited by famous personalities anytime in the year.

Shri Siddhivinayak Temple Mumbai is unique and special

The idol of Lord Ganesha has four hands in this temple. The upper right hand has a lotus, and the upper left hand has an ax. The bottom right hand has a garland of pearls, and the left hand has a bowl full of Modak. Lord Ganesha has his two wives by his side, Riddhi and Siddhi. Unlike other Ganesh temples, Siddhivinayak Ganpati's idol has its trunk to the right side and not the left. Devotees believe it to be a rare manifestation. The most surprising part is that the idol of Siddhivinayak consists of a single Blackstone. Some stories explain that this idol is self- manifested, and no one carved it.
Several devotees profess that Shree Siddhivinayak makes your wishes come true if you pray to Bappa with an honest heart. Apart from its historical significance, you can also get spiritual vibes while being in the temple as it is under spiritual vibrations throughout the day.

Why does Lord Ganesha want modak as prasad during Shree Siddhivinayak Aarti?

As per a puranic story, Lord Shiva, with his family, Goddess Parvati and Lord Ganesha, visited a lady named Anusuya in the forest. Lord Shiva said to this lady that he would honor the food once she satiates Ganesha's hunger. No food was able to fill Lord Ganesha's stomach. Helpless Anusaya gave him a single sweet, which made Lord Ganesha burp.
The moment Lord Ganesha burped, even Lord Shiva burped, not once but twenty-one times. This story suggests that if you can please Lord Ganesha, you will please Lord Shiva. The sweet happened to be modak, and it has become a mandatory offering to Lord Ganesha. Apart from the story, modak has become a favorite Indian sweet dish for many people. Different parts of countries have their recipes and flavors when they make this offering to Lord Ganesha.
During Ganesh Chaturthi, many devotees in Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, and so on prepare 21 modaks bhogas. Specially in Maharashtra, Ganesh Chaturthi is one of the biggest festivals in this state; you will come across various types of modaks prepared by the locals as offerings and later served as prasad, the sanctified food.
