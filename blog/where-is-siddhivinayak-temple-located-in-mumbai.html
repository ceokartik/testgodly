Let,s have a look at the history of Siddhivinayak Temple Mumbai.

Siddhivinayak mandir's construction took place in the 18th century. A contractor named Laxman Vithu Patil constructed this massive shrine. Deubai Patil funded this project. In a nutshell, Laxman Vithu and Deubai Patil built this temple.
Who was Deubai Patil? She was a generous and rich woman belonging to the Agri Samaj who built and renovated the whole temple. The lady came up with the idea of erecting this temple during her prayer. As she was childless, she prayed to Lord Ganesha, and her wish came true.
Another incident that took place 125 years ago is vital in understanding the history of the temple. A Hindu saint Akkalkot Swami Samarth ordered his disciple Ramkrishna Jambhekar Maharaj to bury two idols in front of the temple's deity. This was carried out based on a prophecy which the saint was aware of. The prophecy was that a mandar tree would grow at the same spot after 21 years.
And indeed, after 21 years, a mandar tree grew on the same spot. The realization of this prophecy affirmed the devotees’ faith, and since then, the number of people visiting the temple increased in multiple folds.

Let's have a look at the architecture of Siddhivinayak Temple Mumbai

If you compare the temple's image as it is today with its first construction, you will find a massive difference in appearance. The original temple was just a 3.6m x 3.6m square brick structure with a dome-shaped tower. The temple complex was quite large initially, close to 2550 sq. meters. The sanctum has wooden doors at the entry point. This door has the images of Ashtavinayak (eight manifestations of Lord Ganesha).
This vast space consisted of a lake, a rest house, and living quarters for Siddhivinayak Ganapati mandir's caretakers. The lake was at the eastern and southern part of the complex, about 30 x 40 sq mt, to fight water scarcity, but this land does not belong to the temple as things stand now. The playground near Siddhivinayak temple has initially been the lake used for other purposes. A small Hanuman shrine is also present inside the temple complex. This Hanuman temple came into existence when workers found Shree Hanuman's idol while working for the road expansion project. The head priest of Siddhivinayak mandir brought the idol and built a temple for it in the Siddhivinayak temple complex.

Lord Ganesha's idol in Siddhivinayak mandir

Lord Ganesha's statue is in the center under the dome-shaped tower, gold plated. This idol consists of a single giant stone. Two idols are on either side of Ganesha's idol. The idols of Riddhi and Siddhi goddesses (wives of Lord Ganesh) are present behind Lord Ganesha, and that's the main reason this temple is called Siddhivinayak Ganpati Temple.

Significance of the position of the trunk of Lord Ganesha

Lord Ganesha's idol, which is present in Siddhivinayak temple, has unique features. The trunk’s direction is right instead of what we generally see, which is to the left. Siddhi is one of the two wives of Ganpati, and she resides on the right side of Ganesha while Riddhi on the right.
“Siddhi” means to attain freedom from worldly pleasures. This state of freedom is called ‘moksha.’ So, the Siddhivinayak’s trunk faces right- because it is the deity that can grant you “moksha.”
Such a rare idol requires a particular type of worship practice. Worshipping the right-sided trunk of Lord Ganesha’s idol can only be done after completing all religious requirements and following a strict code of conduct. Devotees find themselves quite lucky to worship this idol of Ganesha. According to Hindu mythology, the human body is home to two energy channels- called “Nadi.” On the right side lies the Pingala Nadi, which represents the sun. On the left side lies the Ida Nadi.
Pingala is associated with vitality and strength- essential attributes that help humans achieve their goals. The Siddhivinayak deity does precisely that- it
enables you to achieve your dreams, and the right-ward turn of its trunk symbolizes that it embodies the same pranic energy as the Pingala Nadi.

Flight

If you are traveling from a different city/state/country, then it's only a 30-60 minute drive to Siddhivinayak temple from the domestic and international airport. Once you land, take the route to South Mumbai by entering the city
through Bandra and continue till Worli. It's better if you opt for the Bandra- Worli sea link to beat traffic. Once you reach Worli, the road takes you through Mahim and Shivaji Park, which leads you to Prabhadevi.

Road

If you are traveling from the opposite side of the Flight route, then drive from Tardeo to Kemps Corner, leading to Prabhadevi passing Haji Ali and Worli Naka to reach Siddhivinayak mandir. It should not be challenging to travel as most taxi drivers know this route as it's a pretty famous path in Mumbai.

Train

Siddhivinayak temple is quite close to Dadar railway station. This means that visitors from both the Western and Central railway can easily reach the temple through Dadar station. From the Dadar station, it's a 10-minute ride to the temple, depending on traffic. Post lockdown Rules to visit Shree Siddhivinayak Temple

Since Covid-19 has forced us to go under lockdown, leading to public places being closed, devotees cannot visit temples and perform poojas and aartis. As the restrictions are no longer in function, Siddhivinayak temple came up with its own set of rules and regulations for the public to follow to take Lord Ganesha's blessings without breaking any protocols.

  • People have to book their darshan online.
  • Show QRcode at the entry gates to enter the temple premises.
  • The timing for darshan will be from 8 am - 9 pm.
  • Offline registration is available at the counter outside the temple premises.
  • People should follow safety precautions such as wearing masks and social distancing to avoid the spread of COVID-19.
  • Only people with average body temperatures will be allowed inside the temple.
  • One hundred people are the maximum capacity of the temple according to safety rules.
  • Children below the age of 10 and senior citizens above 65 can’t enter the premises.
